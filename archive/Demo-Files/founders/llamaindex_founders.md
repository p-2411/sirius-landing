# Founders — LlamaIndex

## Background
**Jerry Liu** — CEO and co-founder. Previously a research engineer and ML researcher at Uber AI, where he worked on machine learning systems, NLP, and large-scale data pipelines. He launched LlamaIndex (originally GPT Index) in late 2022 as an open-source library focused on data indexing and retrieval for LLMs, and it rapidly became the go-to framework for RAG applications.

**Simon Suo** — Co-founder. ML researcher background with experience in academic and applied machine learning. Contributed to the foundational technical architecture of LlamaIndex's indexing and retrieval systems.

## Previous Companies

## Domain Fit
Strong. Jerry's background at Uber AI in NLP and large-scale data pipelines maps directly onto LlamaIndex's core value proposition: connecting enterprise data to LLMs in a reliable, structured way. His ability to rapidly ship research-backed features (hybrid search, re-ranking, knowledge graphs) demonstrates that technical expertise is translating into product quality.

## Why They Might Be Unusually Strong
- Launched LlamaIndex at the precise moment the RAG architecture was emerging as the dominant enterprise AI pattern, capturing first-mover mindshare
- Jerry's prolific technical content creation (blog posts, Twitter, talks) has built exceptional community trust and developer brand awareness at very low CAC
- The team's academic and research orientation drives genuinely differentiated technical capabilities (index types, evaluation frameworks) versus competitors who ship faster but with less rigor
- LlamaHub's community-driven connector ecosystem creates a contributor flywheel that extends the product's reach without proportional headcount

## Red Flags
- Two-founder team with relatively limited prior startup leadership experience — scaling from open-source project to enterprise SaaS company requires a significant organizational step-up
- $8.5M seed is lean for the competitive intensity of the space; fundraising and burn discipline will be critical
- Long-context LLMs represent a genuine existential risk to naive RAG architectures — the team must stay ahead of this architectural shift or risk being disrupted
- LangChain's overlapping capabilities and larger ecosystem could crowd out LlamaIndex in the "default framework" selection for new projects
- Monetization model (LlamaCloud) is still early; the gap between open-source popularity and commercial revenue is not yet closed
