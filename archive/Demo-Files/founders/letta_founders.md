# Founders — Letta

## Background
**Charles Packer** — Co-founder. PhD researcher at UC Berkeley RISE Lab, one of the world's leading academic centers for systems research and AI infrastructure. He is the lead author of the MemGPT paper ("MemGPT: Towards LLMs as Operating Systems"), which introduced the tiered memory management architecture for LLM agents and became one of the most widely cited papers in the agent memory space. Charles's research directly bridges operating systems theory and LLM application design.

**Sarah Wooders** — Co-founder. Also from UC Berkeley, with a research background in distributed systems and AI infrastructure. Brings complementary expertise in the systems and infrastructure layer that underlies Letta's memory architecture.

## Previous Companies
- Both founders come primarily from academia (UC Berkeley) rather than industry. Letta (via MemGPT) is their first commercial venture, born directly from their research.

## Technical Credibility
- The MemGPT paper is genuinely novel research that introduced concepts (virtual context management, hierarchical memory for LLMs) that have influenced the entire agent memory space
- Charles Packer's operating systems background gives the team a conceptual and architectural advantage — thinking about LLM memory through the lens of OS virtual memory management is both creative and technically rigorous
- UC Berkeley RISE Lab pedigree is among the strongest in systems and AI research; alumni have founded Apache Spark, Ray, and other foundational AI infrastructure tools
- Published, peer-reviewed research provides a level of technical credibility that few startup founders in this space can claim

## Public Writing / Posts
- Charles Packer is active on Twitter/X and LinkedIn, sharing research updates, memory architecture insights, and product announcements
- The MemGPT paper has been widely cited and discussed across AI research communities (arXiv, Twitter, conference talks)
- The team publishes technical blog content on the Letta website covering memory architecture, benchmarks, and agent design patterns

## GitHub Activity
- Primary repository: https://github.com/cpacker/MemGPT (now transitioning to Letta repos)
- MemGPT accumulated a significant number of GitHub stars following the paper's release
- Active maintainer community with regular releases and responsive issue tracking
- Research code is well-documented, reflecting the academic tradition of reproducibility

## Domain Fit
Extremely high theoretical fit. The MemGPT research directly addresses the core problem Letta is commercializing, and the UC Berkeley systems research background provides deep technical grounding for building production memory infrastructure. The question is whether the team can successfully translate research excellence into commercial software product execution.

## Why They Might Be Unusually Strong
- UC Berkeley RISE Lab has an exceptional track record of spinning out foundational infrastructure companies — being from this environment is a strong signal
- The MemGPT paper gave Letta something most startups cannot buy: authentic intellectual origin of an important idea. They are not riding a trend; they arguably helped create it.
- Research-grounded founders tend to build more technically defensible products — their memory architecture is more sophisticated than competitors' simpler implementations
- Strong academic network provides access to talent, advisors, and early adopters in the research community

## Red Flags
- First-time founders transitioning from academic research to commercial software — go-to-market, sales, pricing, and customer success are all new disciplines
- Seed stage with limited commercial traction validated; the gap between research paper popularity and product revenue is significant
- Risk of being "too academic" — building elegant research systems rather than pragmatic, customer-prioritized products
- Potential for well-funded competitors (Mem0 raised $24M; Zep has enterprise traction) to out-execute on commercial product development
- Rebranding from MemGPT to Letta risks losing the brand equity built around the research paper, while the commercial Letta brand is still new
