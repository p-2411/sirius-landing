# Founders — Weights & Biases

## Background
**Lukas Biewald** — CEO and co-founder. Previously founder and CEO of Figure Eight (formerly CrowdFlower), a data labeling and human-in-the-loop ML platform that was acquired by Appen in 2019 for ~$300M. He also served as a data scientist at Kaggle early in its history. Serial entrepreneur with a rare combination of ML domain depth and company-building experience.

**Chris Van Pelt** — Co-founder. Also previously at Figure Eight (CrowdFlower) and has a background in building developer-facing data and ML tools. Deep experience with the operational challenges of ML pipelines.

**Shawn Lewis** — Co-founder. Engineering background with experience building scalable infrastructure systems. Has played a key role in W&B's technical architecture.

## Previous Companies
- **Figure Eight / CrowdFlower** (Lukas Biewald and Chris Van Pelt) — Built and scaled a leading data labeling platform, successfully exited to Appen. This experience gave both founders a deep understanding of the ML data flywheel and the enterprise ML tooling market.
- **Kaggle** — Lukas Biewald was an early employee, giving him direct insight into how data scientists work and what tools they need.

## Technical Credibility
- Lukas has been building ML tooling products since before deep learning became mainstream, giving the team a decade-plus head start on understanding practitioner needs
- W&B's SDK is praised by ML engineers for its elegance, flexibility, and low friction — evidence of a product team with deep empathy for developer workflows
- The team has successfully expanded from experiment tracking to artifact management, model registry, and now LLM observability (Weave), demonstrating sustained technical product execution

## Public Writing / Posts
- Lukas Biewald is active on Twitter/X and LinkedIn, regularly sharing thoughts on AI, model development practices, and the ML tooling ecosystem
- W&B publishes a well-regarded ML research newsletter and blog covering trends in ML engineering
- Lukas has given keynote talks at major ML conferences (NeurIPS, PyTorch Developer Day, etc.)

## GitHub Activity
- W&B SDK is open source and actively maintained: https://github.com/wandb/wandb
- Strong contributor community around the SDK with frequent releases
- The team also maintains open-source educational resources and example repositories

## Domain Fit
Exceptional. The founding team has lived the exact problem W&B solves — at CrowdFlower/Figure Eight, they experienced the chaos of managing ML experiments, datasets, and model iterations at scale. This is a company built by ML practitioners for ML practitioners, which explains the product's unusually strong developer adoption.

## Why They Might Be Unusually Strong
- Only team in the space with a prior successful exit in the ML tooling category, providing both operational credibility and investor trust
- Lukas's Kaggle background gave the team an early, deep understanding of data science culture and tooling preferences
- Figure Eight's enterprise sales experience means the team knows how to close and retain large ML contracts
- Built a beloved developer brand that has survived the generational shift from classical ML to deep learning to LLMs — rare durability in a fast-moving space

## Red Flags
- The shift to LLMs means some of W&B's core experiment tracking value proposition is less directly applicable to inference-heavy LLM products; the Weave bet must pay off
- At Series C scale, the company faces pressure to grow faster, which can strain a product culture that prioritizes developer experience
- MLflow's dominance in open-source and Databricks's enterprise reach are persistent competitive threats that could commoditize parts of W&B's stack
- No clear IPO timeline disclosed; investors may face longer-than-expected hold periods
