## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** Not slop at the hero — the context-aware viewfinder-reticle cursor (mix-blend-mode:difference, recolors by underlying bg) and the scroll-driven "how it works" index where titles ignite #555→#fff are genuine, hard-to-fake craft. Monochrome discipline holds. BUT the back half reaches for reflex: the "For everyone" card grid (lines 152-168) is three structurally identical cards, and 01/02/03 numbering is reused there (lines 154/159/164) on items that are NOT sequential. The italic-gray second line ("for your business" / "on your team" / "on Sirus") is voice once, template by the third.

**Deterministic scan:** detect.mjs exit 2, 4 findings (3 warning, 1 advisory): overused-font Inter (×2, lines 9 + 44 — double-counts one font), single-font (only Inter, no display/body pairing), numbered-section-markers (01/02/03). No criticals. All confirmed real; no false positives (overused-font is redundant, not wrong).

**Visual overlays:** Unavailable — no browser automation tool present. No user-visible overlay.

## Overall Impression

A genuinely design-forward page that earns its register in the hero and the "how it works" loop, then sags in the middle (templated cards) and under-delivers at the single most important moment: the conversion. Biggest opportunity = harden the contact moment + fix the credibility landmines (brand spelling, broken copy, no reduced-motion).

## What's Working
1. The cursor system — context-aware reticle recoloring by underlying background. Real craft, on-brand.
2. Progressive contact disclosure — button → inline field → success, with honeypot + time-trap + regex spam hardening.
3. Monochrome discipline + scroll-driven igniting titles that make the abstract "loop" tangible.
