## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** Does NOT read as AI slop at the hero — the Fraunces display, the gold italic accent line, the living orb, and the dark restraint are genuinely distinctive and on-brand. The tells appear in the connective tissue: (1) a tracked-uppercase dotted eyebrow above EVERY section (HOW IT LEARNS YOU · WORKS WHILE YOU SLEEP · BETTER ANSWERS, SMALLER BILL · PRICING · YOURS ALONE) — the single most saturated AI-grammar scaffold; (2) a conventional 3-card pricing grid with MOST POPULAR badge + checkmark lists; (3) large vertical voids between sections that read as the page floating in black rather than deliberate rhythm.

**Deterministic scan:** 1 finding — side-tab accent border (`borderLeft: 3px solid accent`) in `components/sections/relationships.tsx:39`, on the nudge/email card mock. Low severity (it's a small product-surface detail representing an IntentLane accent), but it is on the absolute-ban list; swap for a full hairline border or a leading dot.

## Overall Impression

This is a good, confident page with a real point of view — not a redesign candidate. The hero and the outcome-led voice ("The outreach you didn't send", "Caught up before coffee", "while you slept") are genuine assets that nail the PRODUCT.md mandate to lead with outcomes in the founder's language. The biggest opportunity is **rhythm and connective tissue**: kill the eyebrow-on-every-section reflex, tighten the dead vertical space, and fix mobile readability of the Jobs roster. These are polish/layout moves, not a teardown.

## What's Working

1. **The hero is strong and un-generic.** "Stop explaining yourself. / Your cofounder already knows." with the gold italic second line, the cyan orb, and warm-gold-on-near-black restraint. It feels crafted, not templated — exactly the "ambition through craft, not volume" principle.
2. **Outcome-led voice throughout.** Section headings are deliverables and moments, not features: "It's already handling these," the 02:00/08:00/10:45/14:30 timeline, "Caught up before coffee." This is the best thing on the page and it's consistent.
3. **Product surfaces are faithful and glanceable.** The Jobs roster (Needs you / Active now / Standing by lanes), the "Your Mac" local-first diagram, and the world-graph are credibility props kept secondary — never hero'd. Strong adherence to the CLAUDE.md "show the deliverable, not the machinery" constraint.

## Priority Issues

- **[P1] Eyebrow on every section is the dominant AI tell.** Every section opens with a tiny dotted tracked-uppercase kicker. On a page whose brand promise is "not a generic SaaS tool," this scaffolding actively undercuts the positioning to the exact skeptical-founder audience. **Fix:** Drop the eyebrows on most sections; let the Fraunces headline carry. Keep at most one deliberate named kicker if it earns its place. **Command:** `/impeccable typeset` (or `quieter`).

- **[P1] Dead vertical voids break the rhythm.** There are ~400–530px of empty black between several sections (hero→learns, learns→while-you-sleep, and the CTA orb floats with a large gap before its headline). The page reads as floating rather than composed. **Fix:** Tighten inter-section padding, vary spacing for rhythm instead of uniform large gaps, and pull the CTA headline closer to the orb. **Command:** `/impeccable layout`.

- **[P1] Mobile Jobs roster is unreadable.** At 390px the roster keeps its desktop horizontal row layout and truncates everything to nothing — "Pr… re…", "I… q…", "De… cha…". The single most product-credibility-rich element collapses on the device a distracted founder is most likely using. **Fix:** Reflow rows on mobile — stack name over description, drop or wrap the trigger pill, raise truncation limits. **Command:** `/impeccable adapt`.

- **[P2] Content ships blank to non-scrolling renderers.** Every section is wrapped in `Reveal` / inline `whileInView` with `initial: { opacity: 0 }`. Human scroll triggers it fine, but headless screenshotters, prerender/OG capture, and some crawlers never scroll, so sections render blank (confirmed: the CTA headline + Download button were invisible until I programmatically scrolled). The skill's own rule: reveals must enhance an already-visible default. **Fix:** Make the visible state the default (animate from a CSS-visible baseline, or gate the reveal so it resolves to opacity:1 without an intersection event). **Command:** `/impeccable animate` (harden the reveal pattern) — see `components/ui/reveal.tsx`, `components/sections/final-cta.tsx:35`.

- **[P2] Muted ink tokens fail WCAG AA at small sizes.** `--color-ink-4` (rgba ~0.40 alpha) computes to ≈2.6:1 on the bg and is used for job descriptions, the relationships "why" subtitle, and timestamps; `--color-ink-3` (≈4.8:1) only barely clears for normal text. PRODUCT.md targets AA. **Fix:** Bump ink-4 toward the ink end (raise alpha to ~0.6+ for any text ≥ caption size) and verify each usage. **Command:** `/impeccable audit` (contrast pass).

## Persona Red Flags

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User):** The Jobs roster — your richest proof element — is truncated to 2–3 characters per field on her phone. She can't read what Sirus actually does. Big vertical voids also mean more thumb-scrolling through black to reach the next idea.

**Riley (Deliberate Stress Tester):** Disables JS or loads via a non-scrolling agent → entire page below the hero is opacity:0 blank. The reveal pattern has no visible fallback outside reduced-motion.

**Skeptical Founder (project persona — high-agency, fluent in product marketing):** Buys the hero immediately, then hits a tracked-uppercase eyebrow over every section and a textbook MOST-POPULAR pricing grid. The scaffolding whispers "SaaS template" at exactly the audience trained to smell it — a small but real dent in the "this is different" promise.

## Minor Observations

- Section alignment flip-flops between left (learns, while-you-sleep, local) and centered (routing, pricing, CTA). The centered closing line ("Set the boundaries once…") sits under a left-aligned timeline — jarring. Pick a dominant axis.
- Pricing 3-card grid is acceptable (3 tiers is within working-memory limits) but is the most template-shaped section; consider differentiating the Pro card beyond a glow.
- Side-stripe on the relationships nudge card (detector P3) — swap for a hairline or leading dot.

## Questions to Consider

- What if the eyebrows disappeared entirely and the Fraunces headlines carried the whole page? Would anything actually be lost?
- Is the empty space between sections deliberate breathing room, or just `min-h-screen`/uniform padding you haven't tuned? Vary it for rhythm.
- What would the most confident version of the pricing section look like if it refused to be a 3-card grid?
