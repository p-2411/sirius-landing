## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does it look AI-generated? No — with two watch-items.**

**LLM assessment:** Not a casual "AI made that." The palette (warm near-black + gold + electric cyan) avoids the cream/paper + blue-gradient default; the Fraunces italic gold payoff (`So it runs it.`) and the editorial copy are genuine voice. Two risks: (1) the numbered layer labels (`01 · INFORMATION LAYER`) read as the standard numbered-eyebrow tell *at a glance* — the principled distinction (only the two real layers are numbered) is real but internal; a visitor seeing one layer in isolation can't tell it from scaffolding. (2) Dark-field + floating-orb + SVG relationship-graph is a recognizable 2025–26 AI-tooling aesthetic lane; the serif + semantic color split push it past the pack, but the lane is occupied.

**Deterministic scan:** `detect.mjs` → exit 0, **5 advisory findings, all `design-system-color`, zero structural anti-patterns.** The detector did NOT flag the layer labels as eyebrow/numbered scaffolding — the two-tier cadence passes. The 5 color findings are all intentional/pre-existing: orb-vignette blacks (`landing.tsx:255`, `tool-orbit.tsx:80`) and the deliberately app-faithful mock-card palette (`relationships.tsx:11,37,62` — `#d9b978`/`#1a1206`, the real app's tokens). Real resolution: codify the palette in DESIGN.md so these stop reading as drift.

**Visual overlays:** Not available — no browser-injection capability in this environment. No user-visible overlay was produced; findings are source + detector based.

## Overall Impression

A confident, coherent page that mostly earns its restraint. The single biggest *win* is the semantic gold/cyan color grammar (information vs operation, meeting at the orb) — the most distinctive, least-commoditized decision here. The single biggest *opportunity* is that the page's most persuasive moment (the Dana/Acme email card) is buried in section 5, while the hero leads with an abstract thesis and an orb that doesn't yet structurally express the two-layer model (pass 2). The conversion moment is mechanically excellent but emotionally bare.

## What's Working

1. **Semantic color grammar.** gold = what it knows, cyan = what it does, meeting at the orb — expressed (not explained) by the WorldGraph, the gold→cyan flow gradient in the LoopFlywheel, and the jobs-roster group colors. Coherent system without a diagram.
2. **The WorldGraph** is the best element: specific named relationships (Dana, Acme, Q3) with no legend or tooltip — shows "it builds a map of your world," accurate ARIA, correct reduced-motion fallback.
3. **The WaitlistForm** is production-grade: two-step, honeypot, `aria-live`, focus management, AnimatePresence + reduced-motion, real API.

## Priority Issues

**[P0] Hero headline can overflow on narrow screens.** `.headline-line { white-space: nowrap }` (sirius-design.css) + a `clamp` min of `1.7rem` means "It knows how your business runs." (~480px of text) can't wrap on a 320–390px viewport — the first impression breaks for mobile.
- **Fix:** drop `white-space: nowrap`, add `text-wrap: balance`, lower the clamp min (~`1.45rem`); shorten the line for small viewports if two-line wrap is undesirable.
- **Command:** `adapt` (hero, 320–480px)

**[P1] Conversion moment lacks reassurance.** The final CTA is a borderless field under a huge headline — no social proof, no count, no "what happens next," no privacy line, and the 2-step form (email→name) is unannounced. For a skeptical founder this is the bare minimum at the highest-stakes moment.
- **Fix:** add one trust line (a count or "we'll email you in 24h"), forewarn the two steps, surface the existing privacy micro-copy.
- **Command:** `onboard` (#cta)

**[P1] The gold/cyan system requires learning, not recognition.** It's only named in two 11.5px mono labels. Deep-linked or fast-scrolling visitors see the colors without the key; the model never becomes a take-away.
- **Fix:** surface the two-layer model at the hero (e.g. the two CTAs become `What it knows →` gold / `What it does →` cyan), and/or a persistent footer/nav key.
- **Command:** `clarify` (hero) — also a natural fit for pass-2 hero work.

**[P2] Operation section: copy leads with outcome, visual leads with machinery.** The Jobs Roster (6 status rows + emoji triggers) is the element most likely to make a founder think "Zapier with AI" — exactly PRODUCT.md's "busy automation tool" anti-reference, which says keep such props glanceable and secondary.
- **Fix:** lead the section with an outcome statement/pull-quote; demote the roster to supporting detail; consider replacing emoji triggers with cleaner glyphs.
- **Command:** `distill` (#does)

**[P3] `--ink-4` passes WCAG AA only by ~0.5:1, and it's used at 12–13px.** Proof line, integrations label, footer base all use ink-4 at small sizes where real-world contrast dips below the computed 4.97:1.
- **Fix:** nudge ink-4 alpha 0.64→0.72 (~5.4:1), or restrict ink-4 to non-text/decorative use.
- **Command:** `audit` (tokens)

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (first-timer):** No category anchor in the first viewport. `landingContent.hero.eyebrow` ("The operating system for your business") is *defined but never rendered* in `SiriusHero()` — the one element that would frame the category for a cold visitor is missing. Jordan infers "does something with my business" but not what kind of thing.

**Riley (stress-tester):** (1) Unannounced 2-step waitlist reads as bait-and-switch. (2) Emoji trigger labels (`🕐 nightly`, `✉️ on email`) in the jobs roster look prototyped, not shipped. (3) The WorldGraph "YOU" label is ~7.5px mono inside a 22px circle — cramped to near-unreadable on hi-DPI.

**Casey (distracted mobile):** (1) The P0 headline overflow is Casey's first failure. (2) No mobile nav — links hidden ≤720px with no menu; pure scroll. (3) `ToolOrbit` has a hardcoded orbit radius (R=180) in a `w-[480px]` container; on a 375px screen the outer logos risk clipping. (4) A 216px-min orb is ~58% of a 375px viewport — large rather than prestigious.

## Minor Observations

- **Unused hero content:** `hero.eyebrow`, `hero.micHint`, `hero.micPrivacy` are defined in content but not wired into the hero UI. The eyebrow especially would fix Jordan's category gap.
- **Dead CSS:** `compare/stats/tiers/local/vault/routing-lead/timeline` blocks (sirius-design.css) and the invisible `section-index` rule (globals.css) are for removed sections — remove them.
- **Maker attribution** (`— the people building Sirius`) is 13px uppercase tracked ink-3 — the most "SaaS-kicker" element on the page despite the quote being the most authentic copy. Restyle to an editorial italic.
- **Mono-register inconsistency:** the LoopFlywheel center is mono while the loop's own section label is the non-mono plain variant.
- **`perEmployee` eyebrow** "for everyone, not just the org" is procedural; the register set by "one gap, closed" is tighter and better.

## Questions to Consider

1. Is pass 1 a resting state, or is the hero blocked until pass 2 makes the orb the literal meeting point of the two layers? The color system reads most powerfully when the hero *is* the two-layer diagram.
2. The most persuasive element (WorldGraph + Dana email card) lives in sections 2 and 5. What if that pairing *were* the hero "what it knows / what it does" demo, with the abstract system explanation deferred?
3. If gold/cyan is the permanent calling card, how does it survive context collapse (a shared screenshot of one section)? It needs a persistence mechanism beyond two small labels.
