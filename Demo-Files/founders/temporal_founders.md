# Founders — Temporal

## Background
**Maxim Fateev** — CEO and co-founder. Previously a Principal Engineer at Uber where he designed and led the development of Cadence, Uber's open-source distributed workflow orchestration engine that became mission-critical infrastructure for thousands of Uber microservices. Before Uber, he was at AWS where he worked on foundational distributed systems products including Amazon SQS (Simple Queue Service). Maxim is one of the foremost experts in the world on durable distributed workflow systems.

**Samar Abbas** — CTO and co-founder. Also a former senior engineering leader at Uber who co-led the Cadence project alongside Maxim. Has deep expertise in large-scale distributed systems, fault-tolerant architectures, and engineering team leadership at hyperscaler scale.

## Previous Companies
- **Uber** — Both founders were Principal/Senior Engineers on the infrastructure team that built Cadence, which processed billions of workflow executions powering Uber's core business (driver payments, fraud detection, trip management). Cadence is the direct architectural predecessor to Temporal.
- **AWS (Maxim)** — Worked on Amazon SQS and other foundational distributed messaging infrastructure, providing exposure to planet-scale reliability challenges.

## Technical Credibility
- The founding team literally invented the category they are commercializing — Cadence at Uber is the direct predecessor to Temporal, giving them unmatched domain authority
- Both founders have operated distributed workflow systems at a scale few engineers in the world have experienced
- Temporal's technical architecture, SDK design, and operational maturity reflect years of hard-won production lessons at Uber and AWS
- Regular speakers at systems and distributed computing conferences; published technical writing on workflow orchestration

## Public Writing / Posts

- Both founders have given conference talks at QCon, KubeCon, Distributed Systems conferences, and developer events covering durable execution concepts
- Temporal engineering blog publishes deep technical content that is highly regarded in the distributed systems community

## Domain Fit
Among the strongest possible founder-market fits in enterprise infrastructure. Maxim and Samar did not pivot into workflow orchestration — they built the foundational technology at one of the world's most demanding engineering environments and then spun it out as a commercial product. There is no credibility gap between the founders and the problem they are solving.

## Why They Might Be Unusually Strong
- "Invented in production at Uber" credibility is an almost unfair competitive advantage — enterprise buyers trust that Temporal has already survived the worst imaginable operational conditions
- Both founders have Tier 1 engineering pedigree (Uber infra + AWS) that accelerates enterprise sales trust and talent acquisition
- The transition from open-source (Cadence) to commercial product (Temporal) was executed cleanly, capturing the Cadence user community without burning bridges
- Founding team has the rare combination of deep systems expertise AND company-building acumen, evidenced by $100M Series B raise

## Red Flags
- Infrastructure founders sometimes under-invest in go-to-market relative to product — it is worth validating that the enterprise sales motion is as strong as the technical product
- The Temporal programming model has a steep learning curve that can slow enterprise onboarding and adoption velocity
- Both founders are deeply technical; ensuring strong commercial leadership (sales, marketing, customer success) alongside technical excellence is important to monitor
- Open-source competitors studying Temporal's architecture could close the technical gap over time, though operational maturity is very hard to replicate quickly
