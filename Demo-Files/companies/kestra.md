# Kestra

**Overall Conviction Score: 6.6**

---

## What They Do

Kestra is an open-source, event-driven workflow orchestration platform that unifies data pipelines, AI workflows, infrastructure automation, and business process automation in a single system. It provides a language-agnostic, YAML-based orchestration layer with a visual UI, enabling engineering and operations teams to build, schedule, and monitor workflows at enterprise scale.

---

## Why It Matters

Data and AI orchestration is a foundational infrastructure category. As companies deploy AI at scale, they need reliable systems to coordinate model inference, data ingestion, transformation, and downstream business processes. Kestra's unified approach — handling everything from ETL to AI agent pipelines — addresses the fragmentation problem that plagues modern data stacks. With 2 billion workflows executed in 2025 and 25x enterprise revenue growth, the traction speaks for itself.

- **Event-driven architecture** is well-suited to AI agent workflows where triggers and reactions matter

## Risks

- The workflow orchestration market is crowded with deeply entrenched incumbents (Airflow has dominant mindshare)
- YAML-based configuration can be limiting for complex, programmatic workflows vs. Python-native tools
- Kestra is based in France, which may limit US enterprise sales velocity without significant local presence
- Enterprise sales cycles in infrastructure are long; 25x growth from a small base may not be sustained
- Competing against Apache Airflow requires significant developer education and migration support costs
